Penetration Ratio

A measure of how much the current year's low "dips" into the prior year's trading range.

A value under 1.0 means the current year's low has not yet broken below the prior year's low.

Formula

$$ \text{Ratio} = \frac{\text{Year}_{N-1, \text{High}} - \text{Year}_{N, \text{Low}}}{\text{Year}_{N-1, \text{High}} - \text{Year}_{N-1, \text{Low}}} $$

Consolidation Years

While bear markets often break the prior year's low, "consolidation" years are different. They occur when the market dips more than halfway into the previous year's range but doesn't take out its low.

These are years where the Penetration Ratio is between 0.5 and 1.0.

Annual returns for these years tend to hover around the unchanged mark, with some being slightly positive and others having single-digit negative returns.
